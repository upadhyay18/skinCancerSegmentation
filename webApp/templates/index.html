Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is regarded as abnormal and irresistable growth of skin cells. There is a tremendous growth in skin cancer cases in the present arena due to the harmful radiations from the sun. It is known that between 2 and 3 million non-melanoma skin cancers and 132,000 melanoma skin cancers occur globally each year. According to the American Institute for Cancer Research, Skin cancer is the 19th most common cancer. In the year 2018, nearly 3,00,000 cases were reported worldwide, with this type of cancer the survival probability of a patient depends on the early detection of the disease. So early detection appears to be the most promising technique for the treatment of these diseases.

Various methodologies are used by doctors in order to detect and treat patients at early stages which include dermoscopy (magnifying lesions microscopically) and biopsy which removes a sample from a suspected skin lesion for medical examination.

Skin Cancer Segmentation

Image Segmentation is the process of assigning labels to each pixel of an image, which reduces the complexity of the image and thus analyzing / processing the image becomes simpler. Using these labels, we can specify boundaries, draw lines, and separate the most required objects in an image from the rest of the not-so-important ones. Nowadays various deep learning models provide state-of-the-art performance in the task of image segmentation. Medical image segmentation helps clinicians focus on a particular area of the disease and extract detailed information for a more accurate diagnosis. In the domain of skin lesion segmentation various computerized segmentation techniques have been proposed, which consume less time for segmentation compared to manual segmentation.
Due to the great success of various deep learning methods in the field of medical image segmentation, various architectures have been tested for skin lesion segmentation such as UNet, Double UNet, SegNet, etc. For skin lesion segmentation Datasets: PH2, ISBI 2017, and ISBI 2018 are publicly available.
