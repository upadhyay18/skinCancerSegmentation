Segmentation Task

Due to the great success of various deep learning methods in the field of medical image segmentation, various architectures have been tested for skin lesion segmentation such as UNet, Double UNet, SegNet, etc. For skin lesion segmentation Datasets: PH2, ISBI 2017, and ISBI 2018 are publicly available. This tested some of the deep learning models such as Double UNet, SegNet, UNet, and the variants of UNet in which in place of conventional encoders we have used pre-trained models, VGG16, ResNet 34, Inception V3 as encoders we have trained and tested these models on PH2 and ISBI 2017 dataset.

Problems In Segmentation

Skin lesion segmentation poses several unique challenges. The image quality has a critical impact on segmentation performance. For example, blurred images result in poor outcomes. The image pre-processing steps also have an impact on the performance. We need variations of the images to improve the ability of the fit models to generalize what they have learned to new images. It is difficult to collect such varied images which raises the need for data augmentation.

There are very few publicly available dataset so it is difficult to test models on different dataset for comparison. There is no such comparison of various architectures available on skin lesion dataset, to identify architecture with highest accuracy. A skin lesion dataset of dark skin tone should also be developed in order to produce a more robust testing model for skin cancer segmentation task.

Dataset Description

PH2 Dataset Description

The PH2 database was built up through a joint research collaboration between the Universidade do Porto, Tecnico Lisboa, and the Dermatology Service of Hospital Pedro Hispano in Matosinhos, Portugal. It contains a total of 200 skin lesion images, containing 80 common nevi, 80 atypical nevi, and 40 melanomas. dimensions of each image are 786x560. Every image was evaluated by the expert dermatologist with the following parameters-

  • Manual segmentation of skin lesion
  • Clinical and histological diagnosis
  • Dermoscopic criteria such as Asymmetry, Color, Pigment network, etc.

ISBI-2017 Dataset Description

ISBI 2017 comprises 2000 training images, 600 test images, and a validation set of 150 images. Additional images are being added to the archive in an ongoing fashion. A subset of the images has undergone annotation and markup by recognized skin cancer experts. These markups include dermoscopic features (i.e., global and focal morphologic elements in the image known to discriminate.
